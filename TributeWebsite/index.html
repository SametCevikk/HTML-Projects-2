NAZIM HİKMET

Early Life

Nazım Hikmet was born in 1902 in Thessaloniki. He was raised in an educated family. He developed an interest in poetry at a young age and studied in Moscow. Due to his political beliefs, he was imprisoned several times in Turkey and spent many years in exile. He passed away in Moscow in 1963.

Career

Nazım Hikmet revolutionized Turkish literature with his free verse poetry. Besides being a poet, he was also known as a playwright and novelist. His works carried not only literary but also political significance. UNESCO recognized him as the "world poet of peace."

Memleketimden İnsan Manzaraları

"Memleketimden İnsan Manzaraları" is Nazım Hikmet’s most famous and acclaimed work. This epic poem depicts scenes from all over Turkey, expressing both personal and social emotions. Its language is simple, yet its impact is profound.
